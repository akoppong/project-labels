Single-code barcode generators ask you to enter one value at a time, download, then repeat. If you have 50 products, that is 50 trips through the same form. For any real inventory or ecommerce workflow, that is too slow.

A CSV-based approach encodes your entire list in one step. Paste your spreadsheet, pick the column, and preview the full label sheet before exporting.

## Why single-code generators slow you down

Most free barcode tools online are designed for generating one barcode image at a time. They work for occasional use but break down quickly when you need:

- 30 labels for a product launch
- 500 bin labels for a warehouse restock
- A full Avery 5160 sheet from an inventory export

The solution is not to enter codes faster one at a time. The solution is to bring the list to the tool instead.

## How CSV import changes the workflow

Instead of entering codes one at a time, you paste a CSV with all your product codes in a column. The tool reads each row and generates one label per row.

A simple example:

```
sku,name
SKU-1001,Wireless Charger
SKU-1002,USB-C Cable
SKU-1003,Phone Stand
```

Paste that, select the `sku` column, and you get three labels side by side on a preview sheet. Add 200 more rows and the tool generates 200 more labels without any additional steps.

## Who this workflow fits

This approach suits:

- Ecommerce sellers with product catalogs in Shopify, Etsy, or Square
- Warehouse teams generating bin labels from a spreadsheet
- Resellers who need to relabel incoming stock with internal codes
- Small operations that want to avoid desktop label software

If you are printing a handful of labels once and have no spreadsheet, a single-code generator is fine. If you are doing this regularly with a list, CSV import is the right approach.

## Step-by-step: batch labels from a spreadsheet

1. Open your spreadsheet in Excel, Google Sheets, or any similar app
2. Export or save it as CSV
3. Go to [Bulk barcode label generator](/bulk-barcode-generator)
4. Paste the CSV into the input field
5. Select the column that contains your barcode values
6. Review the live preview of the full label sheet
7. Export the PDF when everything looks correct

The preview shows every label so you can catch any empty or malformed values before printing.

## What to check in the preview

Before exporting, scan the preview for:

- Labels with no barcode (rows with missing values in your CSV)
- Labels where the text overflows the label boundary
- Any rows that look unexpected or out of order

Fixing these in the CSV and re-pasting takes less than a minute. Catching them after printing a 500-label run does not.

## FAQ

**Is there a limit on how many rows I can import?**

The tool handles standard spreadsheet sizes. For most inventory and ecommerce workflows, the practical limit is the number of rows in your CSV file.

**Can I mix inventory bin labels and product labels in the same batch?**

Yes, as long as both sets of codes are in the same column. The tool generates one label per row regardless of what the code represents.

**Do I need an account to use it?**

Preview is free with no account required. Exporting the PDF requires a one-time payment at the time of export.

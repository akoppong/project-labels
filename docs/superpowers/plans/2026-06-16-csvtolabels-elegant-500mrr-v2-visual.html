Kill And Pivot Logic

Decide with behavior, not optimism.

After 1,000 targeted visitors, the plan either has proof or it does not. These thresholds prevent quietly expanding scope to compensate for weak demand.

10+ people pay Continue. Build only the next feature paying users request repeatedly.
One-time buys, weak Pro Make Export Pass primary and add a post-purchase subscription upsell.
Preview use, no payment Improve PDF output, adjust price, or move the gate closer to value.
Low CSV usage Landing promise or traffic source is wrong. Pivot away from the current wedge.

Pivot thresholds

  • !Fewer than 100 CSV users after 1,000 targeted visitors.
  • !Fewer than 30 export clicks after 1,000 targeted visitors.
  • !Fewer than 5 paying users after 1,000 targeted visitors.

Review Takeaway

This is elegant because the constraints do the work.

The plan is deliberately narrow enough to ship and sell quickly. Its main risk is not technical execution; it is whether enough buyers need repeat label exports rather than a one-time PDF.

Why it can work

It reaches people with active label-printing pain and monetizes only after the preview proves usefulness.

Why it can fail

Users may need a one-off export, not a subscription. That is why the $19 Export Pass is included from day one.

What not to do

Do not broaden into barcode types, Shopify OAuth, saved templates, inventory management, or SEO-only growth before paid proof.
